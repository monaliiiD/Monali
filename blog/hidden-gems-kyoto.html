Hidden Gems of Kyoto

Walking through the bamboo forest.

Exploring the less crowded paths of Kyoto was an eye-opening experience. We found a small tea house tucked away in the bamboo forest that served the most amazing matcha.

Most tourists flock to the main Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, but if you take the small path to the left just before the main entrance, you'll find yourself in a serene, almost empty parallel walkway. The silence there is profound, broken only by the rustling of the bamboo stalks in the wind.

After walking for about 20 minutes, we stumbled upon "Koto," a tiny tea house run by an elderly couple. They've been serving matcha there for over 40 years. The tea was prepared with such precision and grace—it felt like watching a performance.

"The true charm of Kyoto reveals itself only when you step off the main path."

We spent the rest of the afternoon exploring the nearby temples that weren't on the main tourist map. The architecture was just as stunning, but the atmosphere was completely different—peaceful, contemplative, and timeless.

Travel Tips

  • Visit early in the morning (6-7 AM) to avoid crowds.
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes; the best spots are hidden away.
  • Don't be afraid to try small, family-run restaurants.
